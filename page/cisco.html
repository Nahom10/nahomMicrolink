CISCO CERTIFICATION



CISCO is a networking solutions company that comprises over 80% of internet backbone. CISCO technology includes the designing and building of networks used in today s business world. The Cisco Networking Academy program teaches valuable networking and IT skills to students and workers worldwide who are interested in expanding their IT knowledge or starting a career in the technology industry.

Microlink Information Technology College is one of the few certified CISCO local academies in the country that provides CISCO networking certificate courses at its well situated at Giorgis campus. This academy is equipped with the latest CISCO networking lab bundle that consists routers, switches cables and other accessories. MicroLink CISCO Local Academy is unique among its competitors in various aspects. The instructors of our academy are CCNP s with decades of industrial experience in the area and MSC s in their formal education. The Academy has got accreditation by person VUE to be one of the few certification exam centers.

The Microlink CISCO Academy is currently delivering CCNA (CISCO certified Network Associate) certification. The CCNA credential is the premium certification for professionals who design and develop custom business solutions with CISCO routers and switches. CISCO Certifications focus on both routing and switching, as well as WAN switching solutions in network design and network support, providing you with flexible options for career development. One may advance to CCIE(CISCO Certified inter Network Expert) status by first achieving Associate certification in these tracks. The online portion of the CISCO CCNA curriculum contains four 40-hour blocks of study, called semesters, for a total of 120 hours. Each block defines a separate module.




CCNA Module 1 Introduction To Networks

Course Code : CCNA R&S Module 1

Course Title: Introduction To Networks

Course Duration: 40 hrs

Course Description:

This is the first of four courses leading to the Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA) designation. This course introduces the architecture, structure, functions, components, and models of the Internet and other computer networks. The principles and structure of IP addressing and the fundamentals of Ethernet concepts, media, and operations are introduced to provide a foundation for the curriculum.

Course Fee 2300.00





CCNA Module 2 Routing and Switching

Course Code:CCNA R&S Module 2

Course Title:Routing & Switching Essenstials

Course Duration:40 hrs

Course Description:

This is the second of four CCNA course leading to the Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA) designation. This course describes the architecture, components, and operations of routers and switches in a small network. Participants learn how to configure a router and a switch for basic functionality. By the end of this course, participants will be able to configure and troubleshoot routers and switches and resolve common issues with RIPv1, RIPng, single area and multi-area OSPF, virtual LANs, and inter-VLAN routing in both IPv4 and IPv6 networks.

Course Fee 2300.00





CCNA Module 3 Scaling Network

Course Code: CCNA- Module 3

Course Title: Scaling Networks

Course Duration: 40 hrs

Course Description:

This is the third of four courses leading to the Cisco Certified Network Associate (CCNA) designation. This course describes the architecture, components, and operations of routers and switches in larger and more complex networks. Participants learn how to configure routers and switches for advanced functionality. By the end of this course, participants will be able to configure and troubleshoot routers and switches and resolve common issues with OSPF, EIGRP, and STP in both IPv4 and IPv6 networks. Participants will also develop the knowledge and skills needed to implement a WLAN in a small-to- medium network.

Course Fee 2300.00





CCNA Module 4 Connecting Networks

Course Code: CCNA- Module 4

Course Title: Connecting Networks

Course Duration: 40 hrs

Course Description:

This is the last of four. This course discusses the WAN technologies and network services required by converged applications in a complex network. The course enables participants to understand the selection criteria of network devices and WAN technologies to meet network requirements. Participants learn how to configure and troubleshoot network devices and resolve common issues with data link protocols. Participants will also develop the knowledge and skills needed to implement virtual private network (VPN) operations in a complex network.

Course Fee 2300.00
